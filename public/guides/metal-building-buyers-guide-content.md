## Part 1: Pre-Purchase Checklist

### ✓ Define Your Needs

**Building Purpose**
- What will you use this building for? (garage, workshop, storage, barndominium, agricultural, commercial)
- Current space requirements (width × length × height)
- Future expansion plans

**Design Considerations**
- Roof style: Vertical roof (best for snow/rain), A-frame horizontal, or single-slope
- Door requirements: walk-in doors, roll-up garage doors, height requirements
- Windows and natural light needs
- Insulation requirements based on climate and use

**Size Pro Tip:** If you think you need 20' wide, go 22'. That extra two feet prevents door-dings and allows for structural uprights without cramping your workspace.

---

### ✓ Understand Local Requirements

**Permits & Zoning (Critical - Do This First!)**
- In 95% of U.S. counties, structures over 120-200 sq ft require permits
- Requirements typically include:
  - Site plan showing setbacks, structures, and utilities
  - Engineered drawings with wind and snow load calculations
  - Proof of property ownership or landowner authorization
  - Zoning compliance verification

**Wind & Snow Load Certifications**
- Coastal zones: Often require certification up to 180 MPH wind resistance
- Snow load requirements vary by elevation and region
- Your building must be engineered for your specific location
- Always request stamped engineering drawings for permit submission

**HOA Restrictions**
- Check covenants before purchasing
- Some HOAs restrict:
  - Building materials and colors
  - Placement and setback distances
  - Roof styles
  - Maximum building sizes

---

### ✓ Budget Realistically

**Typical Cost Breakdown:**

**Building Package: 40-50% of total project cost**
- Pre-engineered metal building: $14-22 per sq ft
- Customizations (doors, windows, insulation): +15-30%

**Foundation: 20-25% of total**
- Concrete slab (4-6" thick): $4-8 per sq ft
- Gravel pad: $1-3 per sq ft
- Soil preparation and grading: varies by site

**Labor & Installation: 15-25%**
- DIY: $0 (your time + equipment rental)
- Professional erection: $3-6 per sq ft
- Crane rental (if needed): $500-2,000/day

**Additional Costs:**
- Delivery: Varies by distance (often free within radius)
- Permits: $200-2,000 depending on jurisdiction
- Electrical/plumbing: If applicable
- Insulation and interior finishing

**Financing Options:**
- Traditional financing: Fixed APR for 650+ credit scores
- Rent-to-own (RTO): No credit check, instant approval up to $25,000
- Cash payment discounts often available

---

### ✓ Material Selection Matters

**Steel Gauge (Thickness)**
- **12-gauge** (.1084"): 30% thicker, recommended for:
  - Buildings over 30' wide
  - High wind zones (coastal areas)
  - Heavy snow load regions
  - Long-term durability priority
  
- **14-gauge** (.0783"): Industry standard for:
  - Smaller buildings under 30' wide
  - Inland locations with moderate weather
  - Carports and basic storage

**Roof Style**
- **Vertical Roof (Recommended):** Panels run from peak to eaves, allowing rain, snow, and debris to slide off. Essential for:
  - Buildings over 36' long (prevents roof ponding)
  - Areas with significant rain or snow
  - Maximum longevity
  
- **Horizontal Roof:** More economical but not recommended for snow-prone areas

**Coating & Protection**
- Galvanized steel with rust-proof coating (standard)
- Double-dipped coating for coastal/high-humidity environments
- 40-year paint warranty, 50-year structural warranty (Worldwide Steel)

## Part 2: Site Preparation Guide

**Critical: Site prep BEFORE building delivery is non-negotiable.**

### Step 1: Site Selection & Access

**Location Requirements:**
- Minimum 40-ft trailer approach for delivery
- Crane access if needed (outrigger bearing pads)
- Flat area with adequate drainage
- Utilities location marked (call 811 before digging)
- Comply with setback requirements from property lines

**Clearance Needed:**
- Building footprint + 3-5 feet on all sides for construction access
- Overhead clearance for crane (if applicable)
- Material staging area (weatherproof if possible)

---

### Step 2: Site Clearing & Grading

**Vegetation & Debris Removal:**
- Remove all vegetation, roots, rocks, and organic material
- Sharp debris interferes with compaction
- Organic matter breaks down over time, compromising foundation

**Grading Requirements:**
- Grade for proper drainage (minimum 2% slope away from building)
- Level building footprint area
- Address low spots that could collect water

**Soil Testing:**
- Recommended for buildings over 1,000 sq ft
- Determines load-bearing capacity
- Identifies potential issues (expansive clay, high water table)
- Costs $300-800, prevents expensive problems later

---

### Step 3: Foundation Selection

**Option 1: Concrete Slab (Most Common & Recommended)**

**Specifications:**
- Thickness: 4-6 inches (6" for heavy equipment storage)
- Reinforcement: #4 rebar spaced 12-16 inches on center
- Minimum strength: 3,000 PSI compressive strength
- Excavation: 6-8 inches below grade
- Vapor barrier: 6-mil polyethylene under slab
- Gravel base: 4-6 inches compacted crushed stone

**Anchor Bolt Requirements:**
- Placement within 1/8 inch of specified locations
- Typically J-bolts or wedge anchors
- Spacing per engineered drawings (usually 4-6 feet)

**Curing Time:**
- **Minimum 7 days before assembly**
- **28 days for full cure** (ideal before erection)
- Keep moist during curing for maximum strength

**Levelness Tolerance:**
- Maximum 1/4 inch variation across entire foundation
- Critical for proper frame assembly
- Check with 10-ft level or laser level

---

**Option 2: Gravel Pad**
- Cost-effective for temporary or small buildings
- 6-8 inches compacted crushed gravel
- Ground anchors (auger anchors or mobile home anchors)
- Not recommended for buildings over 1,200 sq ft or high-wind zones

**Option 3: Pier Foundation**
- For sloped sites or crawl space needs
- Concrete piers at each column location
- Requires precise engineering
- Higher cost than slab

## Part 3: DIY Assembly Best Practices

**Can You DIY?**
- Buildings under 3,000 sq ft: Feasible with construction experience
- 3,000-5,000 sq ft: Requires 3-4 experienced helpers
- Over 5,000 sq ft: Consider professional erection (crane needed)

---

### Pre-Assembly Preparation

**Organize Components:**
1. Lay out all components by assembly sequence:
   - Main frame pieces (columns, rafters)
   - Wall panels
   - Roof panels
   - Trim and accessories
2. Verify all parts against packing list before starting
3. Keep fasteners organized by type and size
4. Prevents 90% of "can't find the part" problems

**Clean Components:**
- Wipe down all metal with cloth before assembly
- Manufacturers apply protective wax/oil during shipping
- Slippery components are dangerous and harder to work with

**Tool & Equipment Checklist:**
- Cordless drill/impact driver (18V minimum)
- Metal-cutting circular saw or metal shears
- Socket set (deep sockets for self-tapping screws)
- 10-ft level or laser level
- Measuring tape (25-ft minimum)
- Ladders or scaffolding
- Safety equipment (see below)
- Helper(s) - never attempt alone

---

### Safety First - Non-Negotiable

**Personal Protective Equipment:**
- Safety glasses (ANSI Z87.1 rated)
- Work gloves (cut-resistant)
- Steel-toe boots
- Hard hat for overhead work
- Hearing protection when cutting metal

**Safe Lifting & Material Handling:**
- Plan material offloading before truck arrives
- Use 2-3 people for panels over 10 feet
- Bend knees, not back
- Watch for wind - panels act as sails
- Never work alone on elevated surfaces

**Weather Considerations:**
- Do not erect in wind over 15 mph
- Avoid rain (slippery metal is extremely dangerous)
- Plan for shade and hydration in hot weather

---

### Frame Assembly - The Critical Phase

**"Measure Twice, Cut Never" - Metal buildings are pre-cut and pre-punched**

**Step 1: Frame Column Installation**
1. Align base plates with anchor bolts (within 1/8")
2. Hand-tighten nuts only - do NOT fully tighten yet
3. Use temporary bracing to keep columns plumb
4. Check plumb with level on two adjacent sides

**Step 2: Rafter Installation**
1. With helpers, lift end-wall rafters onto columns
2. Align bolt holes - should line up perfectly if foundation is level
3. Insert bolts finger-tight
4. Repeat for all end-wall frames

**Step 3: Interior Frames**
1. Stand interior frames one at a time
2. Connect with purlins (horizontal girts)
3. Everything stays loose until full frame is up

**Critical Rule: SQUARE BEFORE TIGHTENING**
- Measure diagonal corner-to-corner (both directions)
- Diagonals must match within 1/4"
- Every frame slightly out of square multiplies into major panel problems
- Adjust base plates while bolts are loose
- Only tighten after confirming square

**Step 4: Final Tightening**
- Tighten all bolts in sequence (per instruction manual)
- Use torque specs if provided
- Recheck square after tightening

---

### Panel Installation

**Wall Panels:**
1. Start at corner
2. Overlap panels per instructions (typically 1-2 corrugations)
3. Screw pattern: every valley at top and bottom, every other valley mid-span
4. Self-tapping screws with neoprene washers (prevents leaks)
5. Do not overtighten - strip metal, compress washer properly

**Roof Panels:**
1. Start at eave, work toward peak
2. **Vertical roof:** Panels run peak-to-eave (proper water flow)
3. Overlap minimum 6 inches at seams
4. Install ridge cap last
5. Walk carefully - use roof jacks or harness above 10 feet

**Trim Installation:**
- Install after panels
- Corner trim, base trim, eave trim, rake trim
- Seals building and gives finished appearance
- Use butyl tape or sealant at trim seams

### Common Assembly Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Starting without reading instructions** - Every manufacturer differs slightly
2. **Tightening bolts before checking square** - Almost impossible to fix later
3. **Wrong fastener placement** - Causes leaks and loose panels
4. **Skipping temporary bracing** - Frames can collapse
5. **Working in windy conditions** - Dangerous and damages panels
6. **Improper panel overlap** - Leads to leaks
7. **Not checking levelness repeatedly** - Small errors compound

## Part 4: Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Before Purchase
- ❌ Not checking local permit requirements first
- ❌ Underestimating total project cost (budget only for building kit)
- ❌ Choosing size based on budget instead of actual needs
- ❌ Skipping HOA review
- ❌ Not confirming delivery access to site

### Site Preparation
- ❌ Pouring foundation before permits approved
- ❌ Not allowing concrete to cure fully (minimum 7 days)
- ❌ Ignoring drainage and grading
- ❌ Foundation not level (causes major assembly problems)
- ❌ Anchor bolts misplaced or out of tolerance

### During Assembly
- ❌ Attempting DIY without adequate help
- ❌ Not organizing components before starting
- ❌ Tightening bolts before frame is square
- ❌ Wrong fastener types or placement
- ❌ Working in unsafe weather conditions
- ❌ Skipping safety equipment
